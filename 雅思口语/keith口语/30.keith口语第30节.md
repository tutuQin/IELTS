
这份由 Keith 老师亲自交付的全新核心教材名为 **《BEST IDIOMS FOR IELTS SPEAKING TO DESCRIBE FEELINGS》（雅思口语描述情感最强高分习语指南）**。

Keith 老师在开篇亮出了极具降维打击的考场心法：在雅思口语第二部分（Part 2）中，提示卡（Cue Card）的最后一问几乎雷打不动全都是 **“Explain how you felt about it（解释你对此感受如何）”**。许多基础卡在 5.5 分的考生，由于缺乏肌肉训练，现场只能干瘪地重复 _“I was sad, I felt happy, I was nervous”_。**想要稳拿 7 分及以上，必须使用更高级、自然的口语化情绪习语（Idiomatic expressions）来武装你的口腔肌肉**。

以下为您带来全面、详尽的 12 大情感阵营分类总结，并完整收录了书中所有高分真题示范（Model Answers）：

## 🛠️ 一、 12 大情绪场景高分习语与官方原版例句精解

### 🚀 1. 极度兴奋与期待（Excited）

- `To be raring to go`：迫不及待地想要开始行动、跃跃欲试（_Ready and excited to go_）。
    
- `On the edge of one's seat`：形容看电影或读小说时由于情节极具悬念、高潮迭起，整个人**激动得坐在了椅子的最边缘**。
    
- `Bouncing off the walls`：**画面感极强的 7-8 分口语词**。字面意思是像小孩子被关在家里时“在墙壁之间弹来弹去”，形容**精力爆棚、极度兴奋**。
    
- `Could hardly wait` / `Couldn't wait to...`：迫不及待、一刻都不想多等。
    
- **👑 官方原版短句**：
    
    - _“At the start of the football match, I was really excited and I was **raring to go**.”_
        
    - _“I was **bouncing off the walls** as I was waiting to go to my favorite concert.”_
        
    - _“I **could hardly wait** to tell my friend about this new film.”_
        

### 🤩 2. 被深深震撼与惊艳（Impressed）

- `It blew my mind.`：这极度震撼了我、**简直炸裂了我的大脑 / 颠覆了我的认知**。
    
- `It blew me away.`：它彻底把我给惊艳到了 / 征服了我。
    
- `To be bowled over by...`：来源于板球运动（Cricket），指被某人的才华、知识、热情**给迎面击倒 / 留下了无与伦比的深刻印象**。
    
- **👑 官方原版短句**：
    
    - _“He blew me away with all his knowledge.”_
        
    - _“I was **bowled over** by his knowledge.”_
        

### 🎬 🔥 经典考场真题实战演练（一）：兴奋与惊艳的无缝融合

> **真题 A：Describe a performance you watched recently and explain how you felt about it.**（描述你最近看的一场表演并解释你的感受）。

- 👑 **官方满分高分演练文本**： "I recently saw my favourite pop band in concert. For days before, **I was bouncing off the walls**, and **I just couldn't wait to see them**. On the day of the concert, **they just blew me away**. **I was bowled over by their energy and enthusiasm**. I couldn't wait to tell my friends about it."
    

> **真题 B：Describe an exciting event you have been to and explain why it was exciting.**（描述你参加过的一个兴奋的活动并解释为什么兴奋）。

- 👑 **官方满分高分演练文本**： "I recently attended the release of a new film in my local cinema. For weeks before **I was raring to go**, **I was so excited I could hardly wait**. On the actual day, I finally got to see the film and **it totally blew my mind**. The acting was great and the plot **had me on the edge of my seat** all the way through."
    

### 🌋 3. 愤怒到彻底暴走（Angry）

当你想形容某种行为把你气得七窍生烟时，狠狠砸出这 8 组高分词汇：

- `Be beside oneself with anger`：气得彻底**丧失了理智 / 抓狂发疯**。
    
- `Fly off the handle`：**暴怒！** 形容火气极大、毫无预兆地**瞬间大发雷霆 / 突然掀桌子**。
    
- `Go spare` / `Go through the roof` / `Go ballistic`：这三组短语力度逐层递进。`Go through the roof` 指气得头顶直冲屋顶；`Go ballistic` 画面感最强，指**像“弹道导弹（Ballistic Missile）”一样发生暴烈的大爆炸**。
    
- `Lose my cool`：没能保持冷静、失控了。
    
- `My patience was pushed to the limit.`：我的耐心被**狠狠逼到了最极限，最终彻底崩溃了**。
    
- `Make my blood boil`：纯正的化学反应习语 —— 这件事**气得我浑身“血液沸腾”**。
    

### 🎬 🔥 经典考场真题实战演练（二）：愤怒与暴走的情景大实话

> **真题 C：Describe a time someone apologised to you and explain how you felt about it.**（描述别人向你道歉的经历以及你的感受）。

- 👑 **官方满分高分演练文本**： "Last week my friend borrowed my favourite book and then lost it. Initially, **I was beside myself with anger**. I mean **I'm not one to fly off the handle** but that book had sentimental value for me. I calmed down later and accepted his apology."
    

> **真题 D：Describe a time when someone didn't tell you the truth and explain how you felt.**（描述某人没对你说实话的经历与感受）。

- 👑 **官方满分高分演练文本**： "When I bought this software, the salesman said it could be used with any computer. When I realised this was not the case, **I went spare**. Seriously, **I nearly went through the roof**. **It makes my blood boil when salespeople blatantly lie**."
    

> **真题 E：Describe an important journey that was delayed and explain how you felt.**（描述一次重要行程延误的经历与感受）。

- 👑 **官方满分高分演练文本**： "Last month my flight to Rome was delayed for 10 hours. I was so annoyed, and when they refused to give us any free food, **I lost my cool**. When I spoke to the representative, she was so unhelpful, **I just went ballistic**, and started shouting at her. I'm normally a calm person, but at that time, **my patience was really pushed to the limit**. I just wanted to get to Rome."
    

### 🤯 4. 极度挫败与不爽（Frustrated）

- `It gets / got on my nerves.`：这极其**挑动我的神经 / 让我烦躁和抓狂**。
    
- `It drove me up the wall.`：把我**逼到了墙角 / 逼得我直撞墙**。
    
- `It drove me round the bend.`：把我逼得在弯道里打转、**快把我彻底逼疯了**。
    
- `Give someone a piece of your mind`：**极高级口语**，意为“把我的真实想法和情绪毫不客气地甩在他脸上 / **狠狠痛训了他一顿、当面训斥他**”。
    
- `I was banging my head against the wall.`：形容无能为力的极度沮丧感 —— **我简直是在拿自己的脑袋去撞墙**。
    
- `That was the last straw.`：**“压死骆驼的最后一根稻草”**，引爆你的最后一击。
    

### ⏳ 5. 极度不耐烦与无能为力（Impatient）

- `It tried my patience.`：这极其**在考验/消磨我的忍耐力**。
    
- `I was running out of patience.`：我的耐心正在一点一点**被耗尽**。
    
- `I was at my wit's end.`：我的智商和智谋都已经到了尽头 ➔ **我已经无计可施、智穷计尽、彻底没辙了**。
    
- `I was at the end of my tether` / `I was at the end of my rope`：英国人习惯说 `tether`（拴动物的绳子），美国人说 `rope`。指你在海里求生抓绳子结果抓到了最末端 —— **我已经忍无可忍、到了崩溃的边缘、山穷水尽了**。
    

### 🎬 🔥 经典考场真题实战演练（三）：挫败与不耐烦的吐槽连击

> **真题 F：Describe a time when you could not use your mobile phone and explain your feelings.**（描述无法使用手机的经历与感受）。

- 👑 **官方满分高分演练文本**： "When I was at the bank last week, they told me I couldn't use my mobile phone there. **These silly rules really get on my nerves**. I asked why, and they just said that was the rule. **It drove me up the wall**, because I needed to make a call but I didn't want to lose my place in the queue. These kind of rules **really try my patience**. If I had had more time, I would've complained to the manager and **given him a piece of my mind**."
    

> **真题 G：Describe a crowded place you have been to and explain how you felt.**（描述拥挤场所的经历与感受）。

- 👑 **官方满分高分演练文本**： "To be honest, I don't like crowds, so I was a little nervous when my brother invited me to watch a football match with him. The stadium was packed full. People behind us were pushing and **电 got on my nerves**. After 30 minutes of pushing **I was at my wit's end**. Seriously, **I was banging my head against the wall**. I was about to **go ballistic**. But my brother told me to calm down, and we decided to leave the game early."
    

### 🥱 6. 极度无聊与憋屈（Bored）

- `To be fed up to the back teeth`：我的厌恶和无聊已经**塞满到了我最后面的大牙槽骨了 / 彻底忍无可忍了**。
    
- `To be cheesed off with...`：形容因为极度无聊而产生的一种**烦躁和嫌弃不爽**。
    
- `To have had it up to here`：手向上比划到脖子或者头顶 —— **我的忍耐已经积压到这个最高水位线了 / 我受够了**。
    
- `To be bored to tears / To be bored to death`：无聊到想哭 / 无聊到想死。
    

> **真题 H：Describe a time you got bored when you were with others.**（描述和别人在一起时感到极度无聊的经历）。

- 👑 **官方满分演练文本**：_"A few weeks ago I went to a party with a friend of mine. I was a bit **cheesed off with him** actually... we went to the party and **I was bored to death**. There was no music, no food... I was **fed up to the back teeth** and told my friend **I had had it up to here** and was going home."_
    

### 💖 7. 高级纯正的极致快乐（Happy & Happy with something）

不要在考场上只会干瘪地发出 "I am happy"，交替使用这 4 组母语者最高级别的快乐习语：

- `Over the moon`：高兴得飞过了月球 / **快活上天了**。
    
- `Tickled pink`：像被人挠了咯吱窝（tickled）一样**乐坏了、开心得满脸通红**。
    
- `On cloud nine`：高兴得**飘到了九重云霄之上**。
    
- `Thrilled to bits`：兴奋快乐到了骨子里的每一个细胞。
    
- `It made my day.`：这件事**点亮了我这一整天 / 让我今天功德圆满了**。
    
- `It was music to my ears.`：这句话听起来**简直就像音乐一样悦耳美妙**。
    
- `It was just what the doctor ordered.`：**“医生开的完美药方”** ➔ 极其纯正的口语习语，指它**正中下怀、来得正是时候、恰恰是我此时此刻最最渴望和需要的解药**。
    

> **真题 I：Describe an occasion when you celebrated your achievement.**（描述庆祝个人成就的经历）。

- 👑 **官方满分演练文本**：_"A few years ago I finished my Masters degree. **I was over the moon**. It had taken me four years... When I finally got the certificate, **I was tickled pink**. As you can imagine - **it made my day**!"_
    

### 🙏 8. 满怀感激与谢意（Thankful）

- `To be appreciative`：充满感激之情。
    
- `To be eternally grateful`：**在精神上表示“永生永世感激不尽”**。
    
- `To be at a loss for words`：感激得**惊喜交加、一时间不知该说什么好 / 语塞了**。
    
- `I couldn't thank him/her enough.`：我对他/她**简直感激得不知该怎么谢才好**。
    

> **真题 J：Describe a time you lost something and finally got it back.**（描述失而复得的经历）。

- 👑 **官方满分演练文本**：_"A man had found it and brought it to my home. **I was so appreciative and to be honest, at a loss for words**. I invited him in for some tea and gave him a reward to show **I was eternally grateful**... I was **tickled pink** I had got it back and **I just couldn't thank him enough**."_
    

### 🥶 9. 极度焦虑与神经紧绷（Nervous）

- `To be worried sick about it`：**气色极其难看地“急出病来” / 焦虑得要命**。
    
- `To have butterflies` / `To have butterflies in one's stomach`：**大名鼎鼎的高分口语**，形容上场前胃里像有成百上千只蝴蝶在扑腾一样**七上八下、极度紧张酥麻的生理感觉**。
    
- `To get worked up`：整个人情绪变得**越来越焦虑不安、处于极度紧绷的状态**。
    
- `To be tongue-tied`：嘴里像打了结一样 —— **现场突然紧张得舌头打结、结结巴巴说不出话来**。
    
- `To tear your hair out`：焦虑暴躁得**疯狂揪自己的头发**。
    
- `To be on edge`：神经紧绷地**站在悬崖的最边缘 ➔ 神经质、敏感、坐立不安、极其紧张**。
    

> **真题 K：Describe a time you met someone for the first time and explain how you felt.**（描述初次见某人的经历与感受）。

- 👑 **官方满分演练文本**：_"I will never forget when I got my first real job, and I met my new boss. To be honest, **I was worried sick about it**... **I had butterflies and I was getting a bit worked up**, to tell you the truth. Initially when I met him, **I was a bit tongue-tied**, but then I chilled out a little and everything went smoothly."_
    

### 🍃 10. 彻底放空与解压（Relaxed）

- `To chill out` / `To kick back`：冷下身心 / 把脚翘起来彻底放松。
    
- `To put your feet up`：**“把脚垫高”** ➔ 地道口语，指惬意地把脚搁在桌上、享受悠闲时光。
    
- `To let your hair down`：**“把盘起来的头发散下来”** ➔ 极高分习语，指**彻底放下平时职场的紧绷面具和拘束，纵情享受、尽情狂欢和解压**。
    
- `To unwind`：指紧紧拧在一起的弹簧（wound up）**彻底卸掉劲儿、松开并舒展开来**。
    

### 😢 11. 极度悲伤与抑郁（Sad）

- `To be / feel down in the dumps`：`dumps` 意为垃圾场，形容心情像落入了垃圾堆一样 —— **极其沮丧、郁闷、灰心丧气**。
    
- `To be down in the mouth`：形容一个人伤心得**嘴角无力地下垂、垂头丧气**。
    
- `To feel blue`：心情觉得很忧郁、悲伤。
    

> **真题 L：Describe an important journey that was delayed and explain how you felt.**（描述一次重要行程延误的经历与感受）。

- 👑 **官方满分演练文本**：_"When I realised I was going to miss my friend's wedding ceremony, **I was totally down in the mouth. It was such a shame. I have never felt so blue!**"_
    

## ⚡ 三、 考官绝密提分大招：反向指标杂耍（Top Tip 2）

Keith 老师在讲讲义的末尾，传授给所有考生一个在考场上能够将你的词汇储备量原地翻倍（Double your vocabulary）的逻辑外挂：

- **痛点**：如果在考场上突然遭遇负面情绪（比如你没考过、出了差错），大脑一片空白，死活想不起任何一个关于“悲伤（Sad）”或“搞砸了”的高阶习语，怎么办？
    
- 👑 **逻辑反转外挂**：**“直接去使用任意一个快乐/正面习语的【否定形式（Negative sentence）】”**！
    
- _实操落地_：你想表达你开车挂科了、心情极度沮丧。
    
    - 直接把正面习语 _Over the moon_ 变成否定 ➔ **"I wasn't at all over the moon about it!"** （我当时对此可真是**一点都快活不起来**！）
        
    - 直接把正面习语 _On cloud nine_ 变成否定 ➔ **"To be honest, I wasn't on cloud nine at all!"** （老实说，我当时整个人**根本就没有任何飘在九重云霄上的感觉**！）
        
- （_解析：这在逻辑上完全说得通，而且由于你现场极其轻功般地玩转了否定否定长难句，考官在大脑里会瞬间给你在 Grammatical Range 和 Lexical Resource 板块狠狠打下高分！_）
